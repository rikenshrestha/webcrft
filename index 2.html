Record Heat Wave Sweeps Across the Globe, Triggering Climate Disaster
The world is currently experiencing an unprecedented climate disaster as a
record heat wave sweeps across the globe. From the Arctic Circle to the tropics,
temperatures are soaring to new heights, causing devastating consequences for
the environment, human health, and the economy. According to the latest data
from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), the average
global temperature in the last month has been the highest on record, surpassing the
previous peak set in 2016. Many regions are experiencing temperatures that are
5 to 10 degrees Fahrenheit above average, and some are even hotter than that.

Rise of the sea

Rise of the sea
Global sea levels are rising as a result of human-caused global warming, with recent rates being unprecedented over the past 2,500-plus years. Sea level rise is caused primarily by two factors related to global warming: the added water from melting ice sheets and glaciers, and the expansion of seawater as it warms.

Climate change effects on Forest

Forest productivity is changing as a result of our warming climate. With warmer temperatures lasting longer, our plants have a longer growing season and more time to photosynthesize. This can lead to enhanced forest growth and carbon sequestration.

Global Warming

Global warming is the long-term heating of Earth's surface observed since the pre-industrial period (between 1850 and 1900) due to human activities, primarily fossil fuel burning, which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth's atmosphere. This term is not interchangeable with the term "climate change."
